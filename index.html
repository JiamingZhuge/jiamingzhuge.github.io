    Research Interests

  • Machine learning classification of Fast radio bursts

    • Fast Radio bursts (FRBs) are transient radio pulses that were first discovered by the Parkes Telescope in 2007. Though their physical origin and radiation mechanism are mysterious, a lot of research implies the two types, repeaters and non-repeaters, are differ in various properties. However, due to the lack of continued observations for most FRBs, it is highly possible that some repeaters may have been incorrectly classified as non-repeaters. In 2022 summer, Prof. Bing Zhang, Jiawei Luo, and I, applied supervised and unsupervised machine learning to certify their different distributions in features, classified FRBs, and predicted the hidden repeaters.

    Features of FRBs, 4 observed properties from the first CHIME/FRB catalog and 6 physical properties.

    In an unsupervised way, both linear algorithm (PCA) and manifold-based methods (t-SNE, UMAP) are taken into consideration.

    Dimensionality Reduction

    Details about the works can be viewed on ArXiv. FRB ML unsupervised: ArXiv:2210.02471, FRB ML supervised: ArXiv:2210.02463

  • Assessing the Detection Capability of the Extreme Ultraviolet Radiation of Active Galactic Nuclei by the China Space Station Telescope (CSST)

    • Active galactic nuclei (AGN) are the manifestation of accretion of gas by a massive black hole at the center of a galaxy. The inner polar region of the accretion disk contributes the most energetic extreme ultraviolet radiation. However, the observational difficulties in the extreme ultraviolet band have led to the lack of a clear understanding of the accretion disk inner polar region physics. According to Prof. Zhenyi Cai's research, the UV GALEX satellite data and were surprised to find that the AGN extreme ultraviolet energy spectrum may not depend on its brightness. Considering that the Chinese Space Station Telescope (CSST) will provide deeper UV observations than GALEX, Prof. Zhenyi Cai, Wenhe Lv, and I, launched the project in order to evaluate the capability of CSST in detecting AGN extreme UV radiation through simulations.
